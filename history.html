Historical Timeline

Ancient Rome (753BCE–476CE)

Rome rose from a small kingdom to a vast empire that shaped Western civilization through its culture, architecture, and laws.

The Middle Ages (476–1300s)

After the empire’s fall, Italy became a land of divided city-states and Papal power that set the stage for cultural rebirth.

The Renaissance (1300s–1600s)

The Renaissance began in Florence, sparking an extraordinary age of art, science, and humanism that transformed Europe.

Unification Era (1796–1871)

Driven by revolutionary ideals, Italian patriots united the fragmented states into the Kingdom of Italy with Rome as its capital.

Modern Italy (1871–Present)

Italy emerged from wars and dictatorship to become a proud republic known for its creativity, design, and enduring culture.
